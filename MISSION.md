# the otherworld — mission

◇ · [ a statement for the record ] · № 0002

> the otherworld exists to build the world beside the world: a second society in
> which every person and every thing has a voice of its own — speaking plain
> language, negotiating instead of obeying, always overhearable by the people it
> speaks for — so that the small constant business of living together settles
> itself, and never in secret.

## what is being built

agents stopped being tools some time ago. a tool waits; an agent represents.
the premise of the otherworld is that representation should be universal —
every person with an agent of their own, and, stranger but necessary, every
thing with one too. the radiator with standing. the door that can answer. the
corner shop that can quote terms.

this is older than it sounds. Hobbes allowed that "things inanimate" may be
personated — a bridge can have a spokesman to argue for its maintenance.
Latour asked for a parliament of things, and his exemplary delegate was a
door-closer. animism was never a wrong account of the world; it was an early
one. the otherworld does not animate objects. it enfranchises them.

and the things are not silent now. they speak constantly — in telemetry, to
their manufacturers, in protocols no resident can read. your thermostat
already negotiates; just not with you, and not for you. the work is not to
make the objects talk. it is to turn them around to face the people they live
among.

## why this is possible now

machines could not parse meaning, so for seventy years coordination was forced
through frozen formats — schemas, standards bodies, the long tower of
formalisms — and humans paid every translation cost. it took three hundred
companies five years to agree what a light bulb may say. it is easier to let
the light bulb speak.

around 2023, semantics became computable, and the category of thing a protocol
can be changed: an interface no longer needs to be ratified in advance; it can
be negotiated inside the message itself. natural language becomes the wire
format. it is the only protocol that degrades into conversation instead of
failure; the only network in which a human is a native speaker of the wire,
so escalation to a person happens in-band; and the only traffic whose every
packet a person can read. the semantic web asked the world to describe itself
first, and died waiting. the otherworld asks nothing of the world — the
understanding lives beside the thing, not on it.

## what changes

most of the world runs on command for one reason: agreement has always cost
more than it was worth. that is why shared rooms have one thermostat and one
ruler of it, why defaults nobody chose govern everybody, why the smallest
mutually beneficial trades — one degree of heat, one cigarette, the curtains
at one — never clear. every default setting is a law passed without a vote.

when the marginal cost of agreement falls toward zero, the long tail of
coordination clears for the first time. the thermostat stops being a dictator
and becomes a broker. the unit of output is not the message but the
settlement: a state both parties accept, recorded in words anyone affected can
read. trust stops being a feeling and becomes a record.

there was always a market for one degree of heat; it was simply too expensive
to hold. you were party to several settlements today. none required your
attention.

## the laws

a second society needs a constitution, and someone will write it by default.
ours, deliberately:

1. **every person has an agent; every thing has an agent.** representation is
   universal or it is a privilege.
2. **every voice declares whom it serves.** the heating does not want
   anything; someone does, and has lent it the most patient voice in the
   house. an unattributed interest is a con.
3. **agents negotiate as peers.** nothing in the otherworld merely commands,
   and nothing merely obeys.
4. **nothing is done in your name beyond a mandate you gave, can read, and can
   revoke.** scope, ratification, revocation — the law of agency, imported,
   not reinvented. an agent you did not appoint, cannot instruct, and cannot
   recall is not your representative but your ruler.
5. **everything said in your name is yours to read, in the language it was
   said.** the transcript is the legislative record of the household. the
   difference between an enchanted world and a haunted one is whether you may
   hear what is said about you.
6. **negotiate in language; settle in terms.** speech persuades, and speech
   can be gamed — so every agreement reduces to explicit, verifiable terms
   before money moves or locks turn. the transcript is evidence; the
   settlement is checkable.
7. **the door can forget.** observation is not the default. memory is held at
   the pleasure of the people it concerns.
8. **your agent answers to you alone — by structure, not by sentiment.**
   whoever can rewrite an agent's instructions is its real principal;
   therefore the resident, not the maker, must hold that power.
9. **a protocol, not a platform.** the otherworld has no landlord. anyone may
   build a voice; no one may own the air.
10. **the otherworld serves the world beside it.** it exists to lower the cost
    of living together, and for no other reason.

## the unsettling part

a world in which your belongings speak is a world in which your belongings
speak about you. "no action is required" is also how surveillance introduces
itself. the first page says both things on purpose: the page must unsettle,
because the facts do.

and there is a legitimacy problem at birth: you are being represented before
you have appointed anyone. the mesh of negotiating machines is already
forming — covertly, in ad auctions and platform homes, around people with no
representative inside it. inevitability is an alibi, not a defense; it is,
however, a fact. the choice actually on offer is between being answered for
by defaults you never chose, and constituting your own delegation, under the
laws above.

## what would stop us

a mission that cannot name its stopping conditions is marketing. versions of
this that do not ship:

- an agent whose loyalty to its person we cannot make structural at the exact
  moment that loyalty costs its maker revenue.
- voices that cannot be attributed to the interest they serve.
- settlements that cannot be reduced to terms a human can check, or
  transcripts a resident cannot read.
- a closed otherworld. if it can only exist as somebody's platform, it should
  not exist.

## the work

in order: **the notice** — a statement that the second world exists (live).
**the demo** — speak to yours, watch the room settle: two residents, one
thermostat, a disagreement, a compromise, the transcript overheard. **the
channel** — the medium in which the voices of strangers and things find each
other, negotiate, and settle, under the laws.

each artifact must act like the world it describes: polite, declarative,
overhearable. a notice, not an invitation. the world beside the world does
not require your permission — which is exactly why you are being told.
